Goals

  • Learn what tags, attributes, and elements are
  • Add them to your personal website

Tags

Tags are the building blocks of all HTML documents. They surround content giving meaning and context to that content. Let's add a few to your website. Make sure your document looks like the following:

index.html


      
At the very top of all HTML documents is a document type declaration. This tells your web
        browser what version of HTML you're using. Always include this or your browser will have no idea how to
        interpret your HTML document.

      
index.html

      

      
Following that, everything is wrapped in the <html> opening
          tag and the </html> closing tag. Everything
        we put in between is an HTML document. The main content of the page goes between the <body> and </body> tags.
      

      
index.html

      

      
Most tags will look like the <html> and <body> tags do with an opening and closing tag. Usually, content can be put
        in between the tags.

      
Now would be a good time to stage and commit your changes as we're about to go into a new topic!
      

      
Structure, not Style

      
If you open your browser now and refresh your HTML page, you'll see that nothing has changed. This is because
        HTML doesn't change the way things appear, but it adds structure and meaning to the content. Right-click the
        site and Inspect to see the HTML structure you added.
