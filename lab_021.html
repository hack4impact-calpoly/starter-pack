Lab 21 More HTML

Goals

  • Update your HTML to include new structural and semantic tags

Add More!

Update the all the pages of your website with the new things we've learned about and that are in index.html.

  • Add an <h1> heading at the top, inside <main> on every page. Give it the class="page-title" attribute.

    • Note: We recommend naming it after the page i.e. "blog", "resume", etc.

  • Add an <h1> heading at the top, inside <nav> on every page. Give it the class="logo" attribute. This will be the large name or logo in the upper left of your website. Give them all the same text, your name, etc.
  • Give all your <nav> tags, the class="navbar" attribute.
  • Give all your <footer> tags, the class="footer" attribute.

Update Your Resume

Your resume page will be where a digital version of your resume will be stored! Don't worry if you don't have anything yet, as you go through your academic and professional career, you can always update it!

  • In your resume.html page, make a <div> inside your <main> under your heading. Give it the class="resume" attribute.
  • In your resume.html page, give all your <section> tags, the class="section" attribute.
  • In your resume.html page, move all your <section> tags inside your <div class="resume"> tag
  • In your resume.html page, inside each <section> tag, put an <h2> heading at the top. Give it the class="section-title" attribute.
  • In your resume.html page, inside each <h2> tag, give them one of the following: "Education", "Experience", "Skills", "Projects", "Coursework", "Activities", "Awards", "Interests", "References"

    • Note: It's recommended you have "Education", "Experience", "Skills", "Projects", and "Coursework". If you want to have other categories of your resume, simply add more of the <section>, heading, and all!

Update Your Portfolio

Projects are a great way to show that you've worked through something practical or built something cool. You are already making your first portfolio project, this website! This page is where you can link to and showcase your work.

  • In your portfolio.html page, make a <div> inside your <main> under your heading. Give it the class="project" attribute.

Blog and Contact

We won't be doing much with the blog page in the Starter Pack, but we'll add more to our contact page in a little bit. For now, they should have most of the things index.html has: <nav>, <body>, <main>, <footer>, and <h1> headings.

Note: Stage and commit! Try to split up any large changes into logical, small stages and commits.
